Project 3

Experimental Clock

The idea behind the 'End of Semester Clock' is to create a visual representation of the progress through the semester. The clock will track the time from August 26, 2024, to December 13, 2024, and display the progress as a percentage. The goal is for the clock to reach 100% on the final day of the semester, December 13. Rather than measuring time through traditional hours, minutes, and seconds, this clock will measure how much of the semester has passed in terms of days.

Project 5

Optical Illusion

For this project, I wanted to create a design that is simple yet effectively conveys an optical illusion in the most intuitive way. Therefore, I used perspective to make a 2D design appear 3D, giving the impression of a ball being sucked inward. I adjusted the size of the ball to enhance the illusion when it moves. It may look simple, but I believe this is the most “optical illusion-like” design that I can create while utilizing my strengths.

Project 6

Data Portrait

I tracked how much water I drank throughout the week, depending on the day. My data portrait records the total amount of water I drank each day and presents it in a way that’s easy to understand using the number of glasses. For example, on Monday, 8 out of 12 glasses are filled, which means I drank 8 glasses of water that day.
